# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This project exists to preserve and promote Dholuo — a language tied to culture, identity, and community. We hold that standard in how we treat each other too.

Everyone who participates in this project — through code, vocabulary contributions, issues, or discussions — is expected to:

- Be respectful and constructive in all interactions
- Welcome contributors of all backgrounds and skill levels
- Accept feedback gracefully and offer it kindly
- Prioritize the accuracy and integrity of the language above personal preference
- Respect regional language differences — no dialect is more valid than another

## What is not acceptable

- Dismissing or mocking contributions from native speakers or community members
- Discriminatory language, insults, or personal attacks of any kind
- Deliberately spreading misinformation about the language or its cultural context
- Harassment of any kind, public or private

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this standard, open a private issue or contact the maintainers directly. All reports will be handled with discretion.

---

This project is built on the idea that a language belongs to its speakers. We treat that with respect.
